Lewis Carroll's Other Works

The Walrus and the Carpenter

The story of 'The Walrus and the Carpenter' is, in one sense, the story of encroachment and entitlement: the sun upon the moon's territory or time, the walrus and the carpenters upon the oysters, which they presume to eat because they can. This book was featured in the animated film as a short part in the movie.

Through the Looking Glass and What Alice Found There

Written as a sequel to Alice's Adventures in Wonderland, Through the Looking-Glass describes Alice's further adventures as she moves through a mirror into another unreal world of illogical behaviour, this one dominated by chessboards and chess pieces. Also a now featured film.

A book with all illustrated works from Carroll

Alice in Wonderland may be a feautred and well known writing for Lewis Carroll, though this book features all of his works and stories in an illustrated book. This book features of course Alice in Wonderland, Alice, Through the Looking Glass and What Alice Found There. Along with many other illustrated works from Lewis Carroll inside this compilation of writings!
